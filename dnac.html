Problem: How can we create a reporting function that will fulfill a variety of users' needs, while also providing meaningful, actionable information?

I'm currently working at Cisco creating end to end experiences for networking hardware and software. There are a huge variety of projects and features that I've been involved in, however for this case study I'll be focusing on how we implemented a reporting feature within Cisco's DNA Center.

Research

At Cisco it can be difficult to gain access to our users and customers. In order to get a better idea of what this feature might look like in the early stages of the project, I utilized our product managers (who have years of experience with users and deep domain knowledge) as experts, and facilitated a design thinking workshop to answer some key questions and gain insight.

Opportunity Map

Out of this workshop I was able to create an opportunity map that helped me and the rest of the team focus on what our primary goal was, as well as break that down into how we might achieve that goal. This helped us align as a team and understand the amount of effort this feature might take to create.

Flows and Testing

After creating a few flows, paper prototypes, and eventually wireframes, we managed to test with some of our users.


While these test were very informal, we still gained valuable insight and quicky realized that users had many preconveieved notions about what they were expecting in a reporting feature.

We decided to pivot, and strip down functionality in order to delivery a better experience that engineering would be able to deliver quickly.

Our original concept was to create a very robust, fully customizable reporting feature. However it became clear that users were more interested in getting their hands on the data and manipulating it in other programs.

After a few more rounds of iteration, we were able to deliver a phase one of the reporting feature that met our users needs and helped them undertand their networks better.

Crafted with careful thought in the Bay Area.
